Benchmarking and International Assessment

Students at Al Maaref American School sit for several international and benchmark exams to measure academic progress and cognitive abilities.

Measurement of Academic Progress Test (MAP)

MAP Test: Assesses students’ attainment and progress three times a year (Fall, Winter, Spring) from Grades 3 to 9.

  • To Be Good in Attainment: 75% should reach ≥41st percentile; 50% ≥61st.
  • To Be Good in Progress: 75% should meet or exceed progress; 50% should exceed.

The Cognitive Abilities Test (CAT4) & More

CAT4: For Grades 4, 6, 8, and 10 – helps understand how students think.

PSAT: Pre-SAT for American high school standards (Grade 10).

PISA: International assessment (Math, Science, English).

TIMSS: Conducted every 4 years – Grades 4 & 8, Math & Science.

PIRLS: Reading literacy for Grade 4 students.

Advanced Placement (AP): Optional AP track with minimum 5 APs.

EmSAT: Grade 12 requirement linked to UAE graduation/universities.

Data Use: Internal + benchmark data analyzed → Action plans developed.

Differentiation: Class placement is based on MAP data triangulated with other results.

Our assessments empower every student’s path forward

Through a careful combination of international benchmarking, personalized learning targets, and cognitive assessment, MAS ensures students are positioned for success across all learning milestones.
